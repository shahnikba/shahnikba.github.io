A **hidden Markov model** has two ingredients: a Markov chain over
hidden states you cannot observe, and an emission distribution that
generates observations conditional on the current hidden state. You
see the emissions; you infer the states.

Three canonical questions:

1. **Evaluation.** Given a model and a sequence of observations, how
   probable is the sequence? Solved by the **forward algorithm** in
   $O(N^2 T)$ time.
2. **Decoding.** What's the most likely sequence of hidden states?
   Solved by **Viterbi**.
3. **Learning.** Given only observations, estimate the model
   parameters. Solved by **Baum–Welch** (an instance of EM).
