## What it was

My first full-time quant role, straight out of the Mathematical
Finance master at Polytechnique. Fixed-income research at SGAM
(Société Générale Asset Management), working on the **BGM (Brace–
Gatarek–Musiela)** market model of forward interest rates for pricing
rates products.

## Why it mattered later

Two reasons this intern year was disproportionately formative.

**First**, BGM was a genuinely serious model to have to actually
implement — high-dimensional, calibration-heavy, computationally
awkward. Writing it forced early discipline about numerical
stability, calibration convergence, and the small print of stochastic
calculus that lecture notes gloss over.

**Second, OCaml.** SGAM had a small but real OCaml-based quant
library, an unusual choice in 2004. Working in a typed functional
language for the first serious code I wrote shaped how I think about
program design: strong types over runtime checks, correctness as a
compile-time property when possible, pure functions where mutation
isn't earned. Those instincts carried directly into the C++ work
that dominated the rest of my career.

## Short but useful

An internship is a short thing to have in a CV essay, but this one
funded my later ten years at BNP Paribas — I moved directly from
SGAM's rates team into BNP's hybrid interest-rate / FX desk.
