## The role

I lead the trading division at Hercle, a European crypto trading firm.
The mandate is broad — systematic-strategy research, execution
infrastructure, live risk — but it fits under one line: **run the
algo-lifecycle end-to-end, from research prototype to money-managing
production, and do it fast**.

## What that looks like day-to-day

Three overlapping threads.

**Systematic strategies.** Alpha research on liquid crypto markets:
signal discovery on order-flow and microstructure features, then the
usual robustness / regime / capacity checks before anything goes live.
The half-life of a signal in crypto is short, so the emphasis is on
iterating quickly and being ruthless about killing what stops working.

**Market-making infrastructure.** Pricing, quoting, inventory risk,
auto-hedging — the same architecture I've built twice before, this time
adapted to the exchange topology and rules of the crypto venues we
trade on.

**Research-to-production discipline.** The gap between a Jupyter
notebook and a strategy that trades your P&L is usually where the good
ideas die. Most of my time goes into making that gap boring:
reproducible builds, versioned data, unit and integration tests for the
pricing and risk paths, structured logging and alerting, kill switches
that actually kill.

## Stack

C++ where latency matters, Python for research and orchestration, AWS
for compute and storage. What's changed in 2025 versus how I ran
Ainemox in 2021 is the workflow layer:

**Agentic-AI tooling drives most of the SDLC.** I use Claude Code as
an actual pair for research, refactors, tests, monitoring dashboards,
and the small operational chores that used to steal an afternoon. It's
not a novelty. The compound effect on iteration speed for a small team
is significant, and it lets a small trading operation act with the
tempo of a much bigger one.

## What I think about most

The two questions I return to:

- **Which of our edges are actually stable, and which are just working
  because the regime hasn't flipped yet?** Every crypto market maker
  learns this the hard way at some point. The answer is disciplined
  monitoring plus a willingness to reduce or pull strategies before the
  drawdown proves the point.
- **How does agentic-AI change the shape of a small quant desk?**
  There's a version of this business where a two- or three-person team
  runs the operation of what used to take twenty. We're finding the
  edges of that.
