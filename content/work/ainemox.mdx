## Mandate

Ainemox was a one-person crypto market-making firm I founded in late
2021. The mandate was simple: build a competitive HFT market-making
stack from zero, deploy it across the major crypto venues, and run it
as a profitable proprietary business — research, engineering, ops, and
risk all on one person.

## Architecture

Five independent processes, each compiled C/C++ with no external
libraries:

![Ainemox market-making engine — high-level architecture](/ainemox-architecture.svg)

- **Algo Trading Server (C/C++).** The hot path — exchange
  connectivity, market-data ingestion, quoting, order management,
  inventory and risk controls. Sub-millisecond latency end-to-end;
  throughput capable of 1000 trades/sec where the venue allowed it.
  Launched per algo:

  ```bash
  ./algo_manager -a aggressive_market_maker_v2 -t BTC-USDT
  ```

- **WebSocket Server (C).** A deliberately tiny C broker sitting
  between the algo processes and the human-facing apps. Routed
  commands in, market data out, and copied everything to the
  database server for later analysis.
- **Web dashboard (JavaScript).** Internal-only. Live order-book and
  own-order visualisation, performance analytics, and full control:
  spin up algos, push parameter changes, kill switches.
- **Telegram bot (Python).** Same control surface as the web
  dashboard, reachable from a phone. Used for emergency intervention
  and routine monitoring. Push a parameter change from mobile → live
  in ~10 minutes.
- **Monitoring servers (×2, independent).** Two redundant watchdogs.
  Caught anything from process crashes to risk-limit breaches,
  escalated by SMS / WhatsApp / Telegram, and could issue automatic
  stop-and-flatten decisions in red-scenario conditions.

## Venues

Connected to **Binance, Bybit, BitMEX, Coinbase,** and **FTX** — spot
and derivative books. Wrote each exchange adapter from scratch against
the raw venue APIs: REST for control, native WebSockets for market
data and order entry where supported.

## Quoting and risk

The market-making logic is a classical inventory-aware optimal-quoting
problem. For inventory $q$, mid-price $S$, half-spread $\delta$, and
reservation-price shift $\xi(q)$, quotes are placed at

$$
\text{bid} = S - \delta - \xi(q),
\qquad
\text{ask} = S + \delta - \xi(q),
$$

with $\xi(q)$ scaled by the risk-aversion parameter and the local
realised volatility. Online calibration of $\delta$ and $\xi$ against
fill-rate and adverse-selection metrics kept the system honest in
regime changes.

## Options on FTX MOVE

Ran systematic intraday market making on FTX MOVE contracts — one-day
maturity straddles that price the realised absolute move of BTC over
the trading day. Quoting was driven by an implied-vol surface
parameterisation and a Black-style pricing layer; intraday risk was
managed against the realised path-dependent payoff.

## What I learned

- A surprising amount of HFT engineering is boring discipline —
  reproducible builds, careful logging, idempotent restarts. The
  microstructure was the easy part.
- Two independent monitoring stacks felt paranoid until the day they
  weren't. Highly recommended.
- Mobile-first ops (Telegram bot) was the single largest
  quality-of-life upgrade for a one-person desk.
