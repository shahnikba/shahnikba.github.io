## The thread from trading to healthcare

The connection between trading and healthcare is identical from a
quantitative perspective: **find signal in a very noisy dataset.** At
Huma the dataset shifted — terabyte-scale physiological sensor data
rather than tick data — but the methodology (signal extraction,
feature engineering, validation discipline) transferred directly.

I led the applied AI/ML team and was responsible for hiring, technical
direction, and delivery of production research from prototype through
clinical-grade deployment.

## Sleep timing and cardiovascular risk

The team's flagship publication used the UK Biobank wearable
accelerometer cohort to ask a simple question: **does the time you go
to sleep matter for cardiovascular health, independent of how long
you sleep?**

To answer it, we had to infer sleep-onset times directly from raw
wrist-worn accelerometer signals at population scale, then run
survival analysis against the cardiovascular-event cohort.

- Deep-learning sleep-state classifier on raw accelerometer signals
  (triaxial, terabyte-scale across the cohort).
- Survival analysis on the resulting bedtime estimates against
  cardiovascular outcomes.
- Causal-ML controls for the obvious confounders (sleep duration,
  age, BMI, sex, comorbidities).

The result — a U-shaped association with the lowest cardiovascular
risk around 10–11pm bedtime — was peer-reviewed and picked up by
**BBC, The Washington Post,** and **The Guardian**.

> Nikbakhtian S., et al.
> [_Accelerometer-derived sleep onset timing and cardiovascular
> disease incidence: a UK Biobank cohort study_](https://academic.oup.com/ehjdh/article/2/4/658/6423198).
> **European Heart Journal — Digital Health**, vol. 2, no. 4, 2021,
> pp. 658–666.

## Smartphone-camera heart-rate measurement

Two complementary techniques, both shipped.

**(1) Photoplethysmography via colour change.** The face is
illuminated during the cardiac cycle by transient changes in blood
volume, which modulate the red and green channels of the camera.
Fourier analysis of the channel trajectories recovers the cardiac
frequency.

**(2) Newtonian head motion.** Each heartbeat ejects blood toward the
head, inducing a tiny periodic head displacement — balistocardiography
via vision. The displacement is too small to see by eye but trivial to
recover from a camera. The challenge on a smartphone is **hand-shake**:
the camera itself moves. We solved it by using the front and rear
cameras simultaneously — the front camera tracks facial features
(head + hand-shake), the rear camera tracks background features
(hand-shake only). Subtracting gives the head motion alone.

## Fall detection

A convolutional network on triaxial motion-sensor windows produces a
per-window fall probability. Aimed at early intervention for elderly
home-care users, where missed falls have high downstream cost.

## Wound assessment

A pipeline built around YOLOv3 for detecting wound regions in
clinician-captured photographs, then computing wound surface area —
the clinically actionable measurement — through a post-processing
layer.

Two things made it unusual at the time:

- **No reference object required** in the photograph. Most
  contemporary systems needed a coin or scale in frame for
  calibration; ours did not.
- **Phone-deployable.** YOLOv3 was chosen partly for inference speed
  on device. In 2018 deploying it to iOS and Android was a
  non-trivial engineering exercise.

Used in venous leg ulcer management to help clinicians flag delayed
healing.
