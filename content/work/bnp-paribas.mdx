## The arc

Ten years at BNP Paribas across four very different desks in Fixed
Income:

1. **Interest-rate exotics & FX hybrids** (2005–2009) — Paris,
   sell-side quant.
2. **FX prop trading** (2009–2010) — buy-side / prop seat.
3. **Credit arbitrage on Credit Prop** (2010–2011) — BNP's most
   successful trading desk before the **Volcker Rule** wound down
   US-bank prop trading.
4. **eFX algorithmic market making** (2011–2015) — London,
   sell-side, on the automated FX market-making desk.

Unusual for a quant to cover four such different mandates. It gave
me an end-to-end view from exotic-derivatives pricing through prop
trading to live high-frequency market making.

## Hybrid IR/FX pricing on CUDA — 2008

The most memorable technical bet was implementing BNP's hybrid IR/FX
pricer on **CUDA GPU in 2008**. At the time, NVIDIA's general-purpose
GPU compute was so new that most of the finance industry hadn't heard
of it — the phrase "GPGPU" still needed an explanation to most trading
floors.

To my knowledge, this was the **first hybrid pricing model ported to
CUDA in the entire finance industry**. The speed-up on Monte-Carlo
and PDE solvers was an order of magnitude or more — enough that what
used to be overnight risk became intraday risk. That changed how the
desk could use the model.

## Credit Prop and the Volcker year

In late 2010 I moved to BNP's **Credit Prop desk** — at the time the
most successful trading desk in the bank. The mandate: statistical
arbitrage on cross-asset co-integrated pairs and credit-instrument
relative-value trades.

The desk closed in 2011, a direct casualty of the **Volcker Rule** in
the post-2008 regulatory reform: proprietary trading inside large
banks became impractical. A great desk, cut short.

## eFX automated market making

The final four years on the **eFX AMM desk** in London are where the
HFT-market-making experience actually got real — the desk-side
version of the same problem I would later run solo at Ainemox.

- **Automated quoting** — pricing engines for spot FX with real-time
  spread, skew, and mid-discovery from order flow.
- **Auto-hedging** — algorithms that laid off inventory to the
  inter-bank market against PnL and inventory-risk targets.
- **Alpha signals** — from order flow, client-flow signatures, and
  market microstructure (book imbalance, trade-size distributions,
  cross-venue lead-lag).
- **Online risk calibration** — adaptive risk-management algorithms
  that re-tuned parameters live as market regime changed.

## What it translates to

Two specific things that carry cleanly into a systematic-fund seat:

- **Production HFT engineering experience.** Eight years between the
  AMM desk and Ainemox actually running live market-making systems
  with skin in the game. Not just research — production discipline,
  risk frameworks, and post-mortems.
- **End-to-end view across the trade lifecycle.** Pricing, hedging,
  alpha, risk, execution — I've owned all of them at one point or
  another. It makes the "research-to-production" conversation much
  easier.
